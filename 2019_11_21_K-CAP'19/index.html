Scientific publishing: still in the Middle Ages?

  • Scientific publishing and peer reviews: communicating and assessing research
  • Large bulk of text in natural language: coarse-grained structures in which scientific contributions are communicated
  • Articles: digitized and maybe semantically enriched

Quality of research: peer reviewing!

  • Takes a lot amount of time
  • Lack of transparency
  • Lack of software integration
  • Lack of attribution for reviewers

Reviewing in large bulks of text...

A model of the processes of fine-grained reviews

Research question


Can an approach for scientific publishing based on a fine-grained semantic model help to make reviewing better structured and more accurate?

Reviews can be made more structured and precise

Core of Linkflows model for reviewing

Sub-research questions

  • RQ1: Does our model help to represent and uncover the structure and nature of review comments?
  • RQ2: How easy is it for researchers to understand and apply our model?
  • RQ3: Does our model help to clarify the interpretation of review comments?

Evaluation of the Linkflows model

  • Applying model: descriptive results on a small case study
  • Questionnaire-based comparison
    • Original peer-reviewer: how would they have classified their review comments with our model?
    • Other researchers: would they misinterpret the review comments on our model dimensions?
  • Comparison of automated methods: are they accurate enough to be used?

Selected datasets

  • Recent publications with open and non-anonymous peer-reviews
  • Journals and conferences in the Computer Science field
  • Articles and reviews selected randomly

Evaluation of the Linkflows model

  • Applying model: 35 reviews, < 1k review comments identified, 421 review comments annotated
  • Questionnaire-based comparison
    • Original peer-reviewer: annotations for review comments from 11 reviews
    • Model experts: 415 review comments (3 annotations each)
    • Peers: 79 responses with overall 431 annotations
  • Comparison of automated methods: 18 sentiment analysis methods
